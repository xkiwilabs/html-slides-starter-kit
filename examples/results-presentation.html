Sleep Duration

Strong negative association with distress (r = −.34, p < .001). Participants sleeping < 6 hours showed 2× higher DASS scores.

Exercise Frequency

Moderate protective effect. 3+ sessions/week associated with significantly lower distress (d = 0.45).

Screen Time

Positive predictor of anxiety specifically (β = 0.28, p < .001). Effect strongest for recreational screen time.

Combined Model

Sleep and screen time remained significant after controlling for all other lifestyle factors (R² = .31).

Key finding: Sleep duration and screen time were the strongest independent predictors of psychological distress, even after controlling for exercise, caffeine intake, and social media use.
